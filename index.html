Dr. Marie Curie

The two-time Nobel Prize winner who discovered radioactivity

Here's a timeline of Marie Curie's life:

  • 1867 - Marie Sklodowska is born in Warsaw, Poland.
  • 1891 - She registers as a student at the Université de la Sorbonne in Paris, France.
  • 1893 - She is awarded a degree in physics and begings work in an industrial laboratory of Gabriel Lippmann.
  • 1895 - She marries French physicist Pierre Currie.
  • 1898 - She introduces the term radioactivity in a published article. She and Pierre announce the discovery of polonium. She, Pierre, and Gustave Bemont announce the discovery of radium.
  • 1903 - She receives her Ph.D. from the Université de la Sorbonne. She, Pierre, and Henri Becquerel share the Nobel Prize for physics for their work on radioactivity.
  • 1906 - She becomes the first female professor at the Université de la Sorbonne.
  • 1911 - She is awarded the Nobel Prize for chemistry for discovering radium and polonium and for isolating radium. She becomes the first person to receive two Nobel Prizes.
  • 1914 - She co-founded the Radium Institute in Paris and became its first Director.
  • 1921 - She travels to the United States to receive a gram of radium donated by the American people to the Radium Institute.
  • 1934 - She dies, at the age of 66, of leukemia, probably caused by many years of exposure to high levels of radiation.
  • 1995 - She is reburied in the Pantheon, France’s national mausoleum, becoming the first woman accorded that honor on her own merit.

“Nothing in life is to be feared, it is only to be understood. Now is the time to understand more, so that we may fear less.”

-- Marie Curie

Marie Curie was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize in two different categories, physics and chemistry, and the first woman to obtain a PhD degree from a French University. If you want to read more about her life please visit: Wikipedia entry.
